• Phasing Through the Flames: Rapid Motion Planning with the AGHF PDE for Arbitrary Objective Functions and Constraints

The generation of optimal trajectories for high-dimensional robotic systems under constraints remains computationally challenging due to the need to simultaneously satisfy dynamic feasibility, input limits, and task-specific objectives while searching over high-dimensional spaces. This paper generalizes the AGHF formulation to accommodate arbitrary cost functions, significantly expanding the classes of trajectories that can be generated. It also introduces Phase1 - Phase 2 Algorithm that enables the use of constraint-violating initial guesses while guaranteeing satisfactory convergence.

• Arbitrary Dimensional Linear Optimization Solver

This article focuses on solving a subclass of convex optimization problem called linear program, where the objective function and constraint functions are all linear. I designed and implemented a numerical linear program solver that can be applied to arbitrary dimension. This solver can be extended to solving an arbitrary convex problem with little effort by replacing analytical derivatives with numerical derivatives.

An illustration of the solving process, where there is a linear objective function, along with both inequality constraints (red) and equality constraints (grey).
